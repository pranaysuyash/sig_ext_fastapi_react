## PERSONA 1: SARAH THE PARALEGAL

### Demographics
- **Age:** 32
- **Location:** Chicago, IL (works hybrid)

### Professional Context
- **Firm Size:** 45 attorneys, 30 staff
- **Practice Areas:** Corporate law, M&A, employment law
- **Daily Workload:** 15-25 contracts/day, 50-80 contracts/week
- **Tools Used:** Adobe Acrobat (firm subscription), Microsoft Office, Clio (case management), DocuSign
- **Pain Point Frequency:** Extracts signatures 10-15 times per week

### Goals & Motivations
**Professional Goals:**
- Process contracts faster to reduce overtime
- Maintain high accuracy (no errors in client docs)
- Demonstrate value to partners (efficiency = promotions)
- Build personal signature library for repeat clients

**Personal Goals:**
- Work-life balance (leave by 6 PM regularly)
- Learn skills that transfer if she leaves firm
- Own her own tools (not dependent on firm software)

### Pain Points & Frustrations

**Primary Pain Point:** "I waste 2 hours every week manually extracting signatures from executed contracts for reuse on related documents."

**Specific Frustrations:**
1. **Adobe is overkill:** Firm pays for Adobe Acrobat Pro, but it's slow and bloated for just signature extraction
2. **Manual cropping sucks:** Using screenshot tools results in messy, low-quality signatures
3. **Repetitive work:** Same clients sign multiple docs - should only extract signature once
4. **Time pressure:** Partners expect turnaround in hours, not days
5. **Quality matters:** Poor-quality signatures look unprofessional on final docs

### Current Workflow (Before SignKit)
1. Receive executed contract via email (PDF scan)
2. Open in Adobe Acrobat
3. Screenshot the signature area
4. Open in Preview/Paint
5. Manually crop, adjust brightness
6. Save as PNG (often with white background artifacts)
7. Repeat for every signature, every time

**Time:** 5-10 minutes per signature × 15 per week = 75-150 minutes/week wasted

### Desired Workflow (With SignKit)
1. Drop PDF into SignKit
2. Draw box around signature
3. Adjust threshold (30 seconds)
4. Save to library with client name
5. Reuse signature on future docs instantly

**Time Saved:** 90% reduction (5-10 min → 30 sec per signature)

### Buying Triggers
- **Immediate:** Boss asks why contract took 3 hours when it should take 30 minutes
- **Seasonal:** End of quarter (high contract volume)
- **Emotional:** Frustration after manually cropping 10 signatures in one day

### Objections & Concerns
1. **"My firm already pays for Adobe"**
   - Counter: Adobe is slow, SignKit is faster for this specific task
   - Also: What happens when you leave the firm? Own your tools.

2. **"Can't I just use free screenshot tools?"**
   - Counter: Yes, but quality is poor and it takes 10 minutes. SignKit = 30 seconds with pro results.

3. **"Is $29 worth it for one task?"**
   - Counter: You'll save 2 hours/week. At $65K salary, that's $62/week in value. SignKit pays for itself in <30 minutes.

4. **"Will my firm allow me to install desktop software?"**
   - Counter: SignKit doesn't require admin privileges. Also, you can use it on personal laptop for side work.

**Most Likely Channels:**
1. Google search: "how to extract signature from pdf quickly"
2. Reddit: r/Paralegal thread about productivity tools
3. LinkedIn: Colleague shares "This tool saved me 10 hours/month"
