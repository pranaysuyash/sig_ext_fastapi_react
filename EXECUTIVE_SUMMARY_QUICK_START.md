## üéì KEY LESSONS FROM RESEARCH

### **From Product Hunt Research:**
- First 4 hours are make-or-break (random rotation until 4 AM PST)
- 53% of Product of the Day winners have demo video
- Votes from active accounts count 10x more than new accounts
- Engagement (comments) boosts visibility as much as upvotes
- Expected: 5K-20K visitors if Top 10

### **From Reddit Research:**
- Need 100+ karma before posting about your product
- 90/10 rule: 90% value, 10% promotion
- r/SideProject most permissive, best for first post
- Title is 80% of success (use emotional hooks)
- Post timing: 6-8 AM or 7-9 PM EST

### **From Paid Ads Research:**
- Bing Ads 42% cheaper than Google for B2B
- Retargeting converts 11.4% vs 2-5% cold traffic
- LinkedIn too expensive ($6-10 CPC) for $29 product
- Reddit Ads: $0.50-1.50 CPC, underpriced attention
- Facebook cold ads: Skip unless you have retargeting

### **From SEO Research:**
- Bottom-funnel content converts 3-5x better than awareness
- Need 40+ backlinks to rank competitively
- First 100 words must contain primary keyword
- YouTube videos rank in Google (double SEO value)
- Expected: 4-6 months to meaningful organic traffic

### **From Competitor Research:**
- DocuSign weakness: Enterprise-focused, alienates individuals
- Adobe weakness: Subscription fatigue, divided attention
- Smallpdf lesson: Localization drove 800% growth
- Remove.bg lesson: Single-purpose excellence beats multi-purpose
- Market gap: No one owns "privacy-first signatures"
